Soslan Burnatsev was born on October 2, 1995, in Vladikavkaz. In his youth, he played football for the youth team "Spartak" (Vladikavkaz). He received education as a food technologist. He started rapping at the age of 16. His first pseudonym that gained fame, "Endspiel," was inspired by the movie "Hellish Endgame." In 2012, he joined the Dope Records association, which he left in 2014. In the same year, he released his debut solo album "Nakip," and a year later, he released the second album "Tyutelka v Tyutelku." Also in 2015, the joint album "Bazdelniki" was released with the Vladikavkaz rapper SH Kera. In March 2018, after a six-month hiatus, the single "It's My Life" was released featuring TumaniYO. Soslan introduced himself with a new pseudonym, Andy Panda, releasing the track "Intro." In 2018, only 2 singles were released: "Kokon" and "Brat Peredal." A video for the second single was released on YouTube. On March 8 of the following year, Dose, who was working with Scriptonite at the time, decided to delight fans with a joint track with Andy Panda titled "Tantsevala." Several singles were released in 2019: "I Wanna Feel The Love" with Castle; "Billboard" with Scriptonite, 104, TumaniYO, and MiyaGi; "Sun" with TumaniYO and Niman; "Zamyorz," accompanied by a music video, with Scriptonite. He participated in the track "Govori Mne," which is part of MiyaGi's debut solo album. On October 18, 2019, the first single from the mini-album, jointly with TumaniYO and MiyaGi, titled "Jumanji," was released. In November, a new mini-album, King Kong, was released, featuring 8 songs. The album also featured Pastor Napas from the group "OU74," Zmey from "Kasta," MiyaGi, and TumaniYO. He directly participated in one of the tracks from the joint mini-album of Husky and Black Seed Oil, "U." After some time following the release of the mini-album King Kong, a video for the song "Rude Mantras" was released. Soslan participated in Scriptonite's album "2004" in the track "Privychka." Soslan participated in the joint album of 104 and Saluki, "Shame or Glory," in the track "Angel." This album was released on April 30, 2021.
