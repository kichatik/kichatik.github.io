Azamat Kazbekovich Kudzaev was born on December 13, 1990, in Vladikavkaz. His father is a surgeon and the head of the "Center for Orthopedics and Aesthetic Surgery" in North Ossetia. Azamat started rapping in his third year of studies. Although he worked as a surgeon, he soon left that profession to pursue a career in music. He has been involved in rap since 2007. From 2007 to 2013, he performed under the pseudonym Shau, and later changed it to MiyaGi. He took his pseudonym from Mr. Miyagi in the movie "The Karate Kid." In 2009, Azamat joined the creative association Dope Records, which included, among others, Endspiel, Namo Minigan, Shuga, Asab, SH Kera, and the group The GuyS. In August 2014, Azamat founded his own label, Asata, which included Namo Minigan and Amigo, in addition to himself. During his time at Asata, Azamat released successful music videos for the tracks "Kolibri," "Dom," and "Bonnie." After a long hiatus, on May 18, 2018, the single "Sonny Loshchina" was released. In 2018, three more singles were released: "Captain," "Sorry," and "Rodnaya Poy" (featuring KADI). Azamat also featured in KADI's track "Colors." In 2019, several singles were released: "Fantasy," "Angel," "Bismarck" (featuring TumaniYO and KADI), and "Trenchtown." On June 21, 2019, the debut album "Miyagi Buster Keaton" was released, featuring 12 tracks, including collaborations with members of Hajime Records: Andy Panda (Endspiel), HLOY, TumaniYO, and KADI. On September 13, 2019, the track "Jamm" was released (featuring TumaniYO). On December 12, 2019, an animated video dedicated to Bob Marley was released for the song "Trenchtown," produced by Castle. Alongside Scriptonite, he participated in the track "NE ZHAL" by 104 from the album "Kino bez sigaret." On March 2, 2020, a video for the song "Samurai" was released. On March 9, the track "Texture" was reissued with an updated instrumental. On April 29, a joint track with the new resident of Hajime Records, Ollane, titled "Touch The Sky," was released. On September 25, KADI released the album "Kopfkino," where MiyaGi participated in the track "Prayers."
